The thirteen original colonies

The thirteen original colonies

The country that is known today as the United States of America used to be thirteen colonies of England. The thirteen colonies became the first thirteen states, after the American revolution. In the American revolutionary war, George Washington lead the army to victory. The thirteen colonies were:

  • New Hampshire
  • New York
  • Rhode Island
  • Massachusetts
  • Delaware
  • Maryland
  • Pennsylvania
  • New Jersey
  • Connecticut
  • Virginia
  • North Carolina
  • South Carolina
  • Georgia

The Louisiana territory

The Louisiana Purchase

In 1803, Thomas Jefferson, who was the third president, wanted to buy the city of New Orleans from France. When he made the offer, Napolean Bonaparte offered to sell all the Louisiana territory to the United States of America.

After the Louisiana Purchase, the land of the United States of America almost doubled in size.

Today, the land that used to be the Louisiana territory has become states. The states that have some or all of their land in what used to be the Louisiana territory are:

  • Louisiana
  • Arkansas
  • Kansas
  • Texas
  • Colorado
  • New Mexico
  • Wyoming
  • Montana
  • Idaho
  • Missouri
  • North Dakota
  • South Dakota
  • Oklahoma
  • Nebraska
  • Iowa
  • Minnesota

The American flag

The flag of the United States of America consists of fifty stars and thirteen stripes. The fifty stars represents the fifty states, and the thirteen stripes represent the thirteen original colonies. Origionally, whenever a new state was added to the union, an additional stripe was added to the flag. However, it was later decided that only an additional star would be added to the flag whenever a new state joined the union because the stripes would eventually be really thin.
